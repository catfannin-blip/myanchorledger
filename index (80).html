The Problem

What an overlooked balance sheet quietly costs you

Most small businesses are carrying reporting errors they haven't uncovered yet. Those types of errors surface at the worst time — and by then, the pressure is already on.

📂

Unreconciled Accounts

Balances with no supporting documentation that no one can explain when the tough questions come up.

🔀

Misclassified Transactions

Personal expenses recorded in business accounts, assets and liabilities placed in the wrong categories, or loans treated as income. These errors distort the balance sheet and make it harder for lenders, CPAs, and investors to understand the company's true financial position.

🧩

Owner and Intercompany Balances That Don't Tie Out

Loans, reimbursements, and transfers between owners or related entities accumulate without clear tracking — and become difficult to explain during lender review or due diligence.

⏳

By the Time It Surfaces, You're Already in the Rabbit Hole

Issues don't show up until a CPA, lender, or buyer starts digging — and by then, the stakes are high. Get ahead of it before it costs you time, credibility, or capital.

🔓

The Same Problems Come Back Every Year

Without structured processes in place, the balance sheet never stays clean. The same errors, the same gaps, the same conversations with your CPA — year after year. And every year it goes unaddressed, the cost to fix it goes up.

🔥

The Ledger Needs Attention — But So Does Everything Else

Executives are running the business. No one has been hired to own the financials yet. The balance sheet accumulates problems not from neglect, but from bandwidth — there simply aren't enough hours in the day.

The Audience

Why the condition of your balance sheet matters to everyone at the table...

For Your Banker

Lenders Fill in the Gaps Themselves

When the numbers are unclear, lenders make their own assumptions — and those conclusions rarely work in your favor. Organized, well-documented financials remove friction from the approval process and signal that you run a tight operation.

For Your CPA

Unsubstantiated Balances Cost You Every Time Someone Reviews Them

A CPA who inherits unreconciled accounts has to sort through them before anything else can happen — and every hour spent doing that is billable. Organized records mean faster turnarounds and lower bills.

For a Buyer or Investor

Due Diligence Surfaces Every Problem

Common errors requiring reclassification on your balance sheet become negotiating leverage against you — and can quietly erode your valuation before you even realize it.

The Solution

What Anchor Ledger's Service Includes

Every package is built around what your specific situation actually needs — not a checklist, not a template. Here's what the work usually requires.

  • 01

    Account-by-Account Examination

    The balance sheet line items that matter most are examined, along with any specific entries identified as problematic. I identify what's supported, what isn't, and what needs explanation.

  • 02

    Matching Accounts Back to Supporting Records

    Checking, savings, credit cards, loans, intercompany balances, and other significant asset and liability accounts are reconciled to bank statements, loan agreements, and supporting documentation, where available.

  • 03

    Flagging Balances With No Backup

    I flag every balance that can't be explained or backed up with a document — giving you a clear list of what to address, adjust, or write off.

  • 04

    Correcting Misposted Transactions

    I identify misposted transactions and correct them where the supporting information is available.

  • 05

    Summary of Findings

    A clear, written summary of what was found, what was addressed, and what remains — organized so your CPA, banker, or buyer can follow it.

  • 06

    Project Documentation File

    A structured set of workpapers documenting the adjustments performed, supporting documents, and the rationale for each change to ensure transparency and audit trail.

  • +

    Going a Step Further — Available at an Additional Cost

    For businesses that don't yet have financial infrastructure in place, this add-on covers the design and documentation of structured processes and controls — so the same problems don't resurface next year.
